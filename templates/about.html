Overview

The PRTR transfer summary database contains a publicly-available inventory of chemicals or pollutants transferred off-site for disposal, treatment, energy recovery and recycling. It gathers information on which chemicals are transferred, in what quantity, by which industry sector, and the reason for the transfer (see link). A data engineering procedure transforms the information into a standard/generic machine-readable structure for future automation (see link). The data come from three Pollutant Release and Transfer Register (PRTR) systems that satisfy the conditions for constructing chemical-based approaches for estimating end-of-life emissions of chemicals. These three PRTR systems are the National Pollutant Inventory (NPI), the National Pollutant Release Inventory (NPRI), and the Toxic Release Inventory (TRI), which are part of the Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development (OECD) PRTR that is constructed from OECD member countries.
