About

The PRTR_transfer_summary database contains a publicly-available inventory of chemicals or pollutants transferred off-site for disposal, treatment, energy recovery and recycling. It gathers information on which chemicals are transferred, in what quantity, by which industry sector, and the reason for the transfer (see link). A data engineering procedure transforms the information into a standard/generic machine-readable structure for future automation (see link). The data come from three Pollutant Release and Transfer Register (PRTR) systems that satisfy the conditions for constructing chemical-centric approaches for estimating end-of-life emissions of chemicals. These three PRTR systems are the National Pollutant Inventory (NPI), the National Pollutant Release Inventory (NPRI), and the Toxic Release Inventory (TRI), which are part of the Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development (OECD) PRTR that is constructed based on data shared by the OECD member countries.

The above figure is the enhanced entity-relationship diagram (EERD) of the PRTR_transfers_summary database. The database schema consists of four entities (i.e., tables) such as generic_sector, generic_transfer_class, generic_substance and record. The tables generic_sector, generic_transfer_class, generic_substance and record have 2, 3, 3 and 13 attributes, respectively. The record table is the core of the PRTR_transfers_summary database because it has relevant information about statistical summaries like median and average transfer amount of a chemical substance by industry sector.The PRTR_transfers_summary database contains 72 industry sectors (e.g., mining of coal and lignite), 10 end-of-life activity categories (e.g., underground injection), and 643 chemical substances (e.g., 1,2-ethanediol).
